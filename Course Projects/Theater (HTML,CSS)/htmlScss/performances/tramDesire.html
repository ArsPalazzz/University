About performance

The play "A Streetcar Named Desire" was written 70 years ago. It became one of the most famous works of Williams, brought its author the Pulitzer Prize, and for the US theater became "a play that speaks of both the individual and society ... Nothing like this has been written in the West for the entire second half of the twentieth century" - say American critics. The play has been repeatedly filmed, it has withstood countless productions on all continents on both sides of the ocean and is still being staged in many theaters around the world.
Valentina Erenkova: “My performance is about the lack of Love. The fact that more and more often today the noble feeling of Love imperceptibly gives way to a cynical, soulless desire. And desire without Love is destructive for the human soul.”
... She arrived on a tram with a strange name "Desire", hoping to get to a place where they love and wait, understand and forgive. But the tram, ringing, drove on. Can you lose a dream and find it again? All it takes is a little kindness in return...
