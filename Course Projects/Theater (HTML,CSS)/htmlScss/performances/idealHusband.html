About performance

The trouble fell on the head of Sir Robert Chiltern, whose reputation in the political and business circles of England until a certain point was considered impeccable. Many years ago, at the height of his career, as a secretary to a high-ranking person, Sir Robert allowed himself to sell secret government information to an unscrupulous stock exchange dealer, which brought generous dividends to both. After the scam, Robert suddenly became rich and quickly went up the corporate ladder. But everything secret, as you know, sooner or later becomes clear. Robert Chiltern's secret turned out to be known to a certain Mrs. Cheveley, who put him in front of a difficult choice: either become a fraud again, or be exposed and, accordingly, disgraced in front of all of England. Not knowing what to do, Chiltern confides in his friend Lord Goring. He undertakes to help, because he has trump cards in the game against the blackmailer. The main question of the performance will excite the viewer to the end: how will the revealed truth affect Lady Chiltern's relationship with Sir Robert, who has always revered her husband as a person impeccable in every respect and as close as possible to the ideal?
